Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper, a pioneering figure in the field of computer science, left an indelible mark on the world of technology. Her life story is a testament to her relentless curiosity, innovative spirit, and unwavering dedication to her work.

  • 1906: Grace Hopper is born on December 9 in New York City, showing an early interest in engineering and mathematics
  • 1928: Hopper graduates from Vassar College.
  • 1930: She begins her studies at Yale University, where she earns her bachelor's degree in Mathematics.
  • 1934: Hopper earns her master's degree in Mathematics from Yale.
  • 1936: She completes her PhD in Mathematics at Yale.
  • 1943 Hopper joins the Navy WAVES, where she plays a crucial role in the development of the Mark I, an early electronic computer.
  • 1944: She develops the first computer compiler, a program that translates written instructions into codes that computers can read.
  • 1946: After the war, Hopper continues her work in computing, joining the Eckert-Mauchly Corporation.
  • 1952: She co-develops COBOL, one of the earliest standardized computer languages, making computers more accessible to non-programmers.
  • 1991: Hopper becomes the first female individual recipient of the National Medal of Technology.
  • 1995: She is promoted to rear admiral in the U.S. Navy.
